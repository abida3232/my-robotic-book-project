# Specification: AI-Native Robotics Textbook

## 1. Overview

This specification defines the content and structure for the "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics" textbook. The book will be created using Docusaurus and will serve as a comprehensive guide for students and professionals learning about the intersection of AI and robotics.

## 2. Book Structure

The book will be divided into four main parts, plus an introduction and appendices, mirroring the 13-week course structure.

### Introduction
- Welcome to Physical AI
- **Crucial Hardware & Software Guide**: A detailed breakdown of the required setup for readers to follow along with the examples.

### Part 1: The Foundations of Physical AI (Weeks 1-2)
- **Chapter 1**: Principles of Physical AI and Embodied Intelligence
- **Chapter 2**: The Humanoid Robotics Landscape
- **Chapter 3**: Understanding Robotic Sensor Systems (LIDAR, Cameras, IMUs)

### Part 2: The Robotic Nervous System (Weeks 3-5)
- **Chapter 4**: ROS 2 Core Concepts: Nodes, Topics, Services, and Actions
- **Chapter 5**: Building and Running ROS 2 Packages with Python (rclpy)
- **Chapter 6**: Describing Robots with URDF

### Part 3: Digital Twins & Simulation (Weeks 6-10)
- **Chapter 7**: Simulating Robots in Gazebo
- **Chapter 8**: Getting Started with NVIDIA Isaac Sim
- **Chapter 9**: AI-Powered Perception in Isaac Sim
- **Chapter 10**: Sim-to-Real Transfer Techniques

### Part 4: The Embodied Brain (Weeks 11-13 & Capstone)
- **Chapter 11**: Humanoid Kinematics and Bipedal Locomotion
- **Chapter 12**: Navigation and Path Planning with Nav2
- **Chapter 13**: Voice-to-Action with OpenAI Whisper
- **Chapter 14**: Cognitive Planning with Large Language Models
- **Chapter 15**: Capstone Project: Building the Autonomous Humanoid

### Assessments & Appendices
- Each part will conclude with project-based assessments.
- Appendices will provide detailed installation guides for all required software.

## 3. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Clarity:** The language used must be clear, concise, and accessible to the target audience.
- **Accuracy:** All technical information, code examples, and concepts must be accurate and up-to-date.
- **Practicality:** The book will focus on practical, hands-on examples that readers can replicate.
- **Interactivity:** The final book will be enhanced with interactive elements, including a RAG chatbot.
